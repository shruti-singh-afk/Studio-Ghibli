About Studio Ghibli

Founded in 1985 by directors Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata and producer Toshio Suzuki, Studio Ghibli has become world-renowned for its animated feature films.

With a focus on detailed hand-drawn animation and thoughtful, complex storytelling, Ghibli films often explore themes of environmentalism, pacifism, and the wonder of childhood.

The studio's mascot and most recognizable symbol is Totoro, the beloved forest spirit from "My Neighbor Totoro" (1988).
